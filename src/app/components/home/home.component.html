BugVision AI

The new era in insects recognition

The Problem

Entomology, which studies insects that harm the cultivation of crops, is one of the main areas of agronomy. And that's quite rational, as the wrong definition of the type of pests in the area, as well as the choice of the wrong method of pest control or untimely application of crop protection cause a significant decline in yields and, consequently, a loss of farmers' profits.

Dangerous Pesticides

Most pesticides successfully cope with the task of insect control. But at the same time farmers should know which type of pesticides use, on which crops and when to protect their field from specific insects. Most consumers want to buy vegetables and fruits that have been treated with a minimum amount of pesticides, which usually consist of a complex of chemically active substances, the effects on the body of which are often either unknown or dangerous in the long run. Many studies confirm the link between pesticide use and tumor formation in people who have consumed pesticide-treated products. A similar link has also been found between kidney cancer in children of farmers who have been in frequent contact with pesticides.

When Machine Helps

We come to the conclusion that in order to reduce the number of pesticides used per plantation, it is necessary to improve the quality of identification of insect pests that infect it. However, the classification of insects on plantations, with the aim of applying appropriate means of protection against each of them, as well as collecting statistics on the quantitative characteristics of crop damage by pests, is quite a challenge for humans, especially when different types of crops are grown on one plantation. It should be added that many species of insects migrate from one area to another, some of them are quite similar in appearance, which complicates the task of their correct classification by humans. That's where artificial intelligence technologies comes into play.

The Solution

BugVision is a software application based on convolutional neural networks technology which aim is to help farmers easily identify insects on their fields, as well as give an information about their amount during time periods. BugVision can be used as by one person (for example in research works) as be integrated with smart traps on fields.
